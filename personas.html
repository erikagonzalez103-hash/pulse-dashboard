Investor-ready · Validation pending

Four primary audience segments for the Pulse Dallas launch. Each is testable — field research is designed to confirm or disprove the claims here, not to validate them by default.

The Insider · Young Professional, 25–35

Stat: ~285,000 in DFW core. Highest discretionary spend of any persona.

Neighborhoods: Deep Ellum, Bishop Arts, Trinity Groves, Knox-Henderson, Lower Greenville.

Pain quote: "Yelp always gives me the same five places. I want what just opened — what's actually buzzing this week."

Why Pulse: Reputation tools fail them — they're past the obvious answers. Pulse surfaces what's accelerating, which is exactly what they want.

Validation status: Hypothesis. Field interviews planned for Klyde Warren Park, Deep Ellum Friday nights.

The Elevated Parent · 32–48, Family-First but Style-Conscious

Stat: ~410,000 in greater DFW. Highest sustained monthly spend on dining/activities.

Neighborhoods: Lakewood, M-Streets, Preston Hollow, Plano, Frisco.

Pain quote: "I want somewhere actually nice that also doesn't make me feel like the only family. By the time something is on the parenting blogs, it's already overrun."

Why Pulse: They want momentum without the chaos. The neighborhood-level signal helps them find places that are growing but not yet swamped.

Validation status: Hypothesis. Interview targets at NorthPark Center, Klyde Warren Park weekend mornings.

The Dog-Forward · Pet Owner, Lifestyle-Centered

Stat: 38% of Dallas households own dogs. Highest specific-need-driven discovery behavior.

Neighborhoods: Katy Trail corridor, White Rock Lake, Bishop Arts.

Pain quote: "Just tell me which patios actually let dogs and aren't lying about it. And which ones are good right now — not which ones were good."

Why Pulse: Specific-need filtering is broken in current tools. Pulse can layer dog-friendly + currently-busy as a real-time filter.

Validation status: Hypothesis. Interview targets along Katy Trail, weekend mornings.

The Visitor · Out-of-Town, World Cup Especially

Stat: Dallas hosts 9 World Cup matches; FIFA expects 250k+ visitors across the tournament.

Neighborhoods: Wherever their hotel drops them. Initially Downtown, Uptown.

Pain quote: "I have one night here. Where do I go? My hotel concierge has fifteen recommendations and they all sound the same."

Why Pulse: No reputation context, time-bounded decisions, distance-flexible. Momentum is the entire signal they have.

Validation status: Hypothesis. Interview targets at Downtown hotels during pre-WC test events, weekend tourists.
